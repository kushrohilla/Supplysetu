# Retailer Readiness Scoring & Segmentation Worksheet

## How to Use This Worksheet

1. **Data Collection Phase (Week 1–2):**
   - Gather data from CRM, support tickets, transaction history
   - Interview key stakeholders (account managers, support team)
   - Complete the scoring rubric for each retailer

2. **Scoring Phase (Week 2):**
   - Assign points based on rubric (see Section 2.2 of main strategy)
   - Calculate weighted totals
   - Assign readiness tier (Tier 1–4)

3. **Segmentation Phase (Week 3):**
   - Apply frequency classifications
   - Assign batch (A: Fast-Track | B: Core | C: Learner)
   - Validate diversity across batches
   - Conduct leadership review for Tier 4 retailers

4. **Export & Communicate (Week 4):**
   - Share results with onboarding team
   - Prepare communications for selected retailers
   - Begin pre-onboarding checklist

## SCORING RUBRIC

### Digital Maturity (Max 25 points)

**0 – Paper/Phone Only**
- No digital systems; phone/fax orders only
- No email communication preference

**25 – Manual Spreadsheets**
- Uses Excel/Google Sheets for inventory/orders
- Occasional email orders; mostly manual data entry

**50 – Email/Basic ERP**
- Email ordering capability established
- Basic ERP or accounting software in place (e.g., QuickBooks)
- Manual export/import of data

**75 – Integrated Systems**
- ERP system with integration capabilities
- Automated data flows where possible
- Technical staff on hand for troubleshooting

**100 – API-Ready**
- Existing APIs or EDI connections
- Proven ability to integrate third-party systems
- Dedicated technical resource

---

### Engagement History (Max 20 points)

**0 – <3 months active**
- Very recent customer; minimal transaction history
- Relationship not yet established

**25 – 3–6 months**
- Establishing relationship; limited transaction volume
- Some hesitation or trial period

**50 – 6–12 months**
- Solidifying relationship; consistent ordering
- Account manager assigned; regular communication

**75 – 1–2 years**
- Mature business relationship
- Multiple account touchpoints; known preference patterns

**100 – >2 years**
- Long-standing strategic partner
- Deep business relationship; growth trajectory established

---

### Organizational Capability (Max 20 points)

**0 – No Dedicated PM/Staff**
- Owner manages all functions ad-hoc
- No designated order/inventory person
- High dependency on single person

**25 – Owner-Managed**
- Owner personally handles orders
- Busy with multiple responsibilities
- Limited time for adoption/learning

**50 – 1 Person Part-Time**
- 1 staff member handles ordering (part-time role)
- Can dedicate ~10 hrs/week to learning platform
- Limited backup if primary contact unavailable

**75 – 1 Person Full-Time**
- Dedicated operations/purchasing staff
- Can dedicate 20+ hrs/week to adoption if needed
- Process documentation exists

**100 – Dedicated Team**
- Multiple people involved in ordering/inventory
- Cross-trained backup person available
- Formal processes & training programs in place

---

### Support Responsiveness (Max 15 points)

**0 – Slow to Respond (>48h)**
- History of ignoring communications
- Infrequent contact after initial engagement
- Requires multiple follow-ups

**25 – Occasional Issues (1–2 tickets ignored)**
- Generally responsive but periodic communication gaps
- Some missed deadlines or delayed responses
- Occasional frustration signals

**50 – Generally Responsive**
- Responds to emails/calls within 24h
- Engaged during problem-solving
- Some friction but no escalations

**75 – Proactive**
- Initiates contact to resolve issues
- Quick response times (<4h)
- Provides detailed feedback/context

**100 – Proactive + Escalation Partner**
- Anticipates needs; regularly suggests improvements
- Works collaboratively on problem-solving
- Willing to serve as reference/pilot advocate

---

### Financial Stability (Max 10 points)

**0 – Distressed/At-Risk**
- Payment history: late, disputed, or at-risk
- Credit concerns flagged
- Business viability questionable

**25 – Volatile**
- Inconsistent payment history (sometime late, sometimes on-time)
- Growth patterns unpredictable
- Cash flow constraints evident

**50 – Stable but Small**
- Reliable payment history
- Business stable; modest growth
- Predictable demand patterns

**75 – Growing Stable**
- Excellent payment history
- Consistent growth trajectory
- Financial metrics trending positive

**100 – Strong Growth Trajectory**
- Excellent payment/credit profile
- Double-digit growth year-over-year
- Expansion plans or geographic growth

---

### Willingness to Adopt (Max 10 points)

**0 – Resistant/Skeptical**
- Expressed concerns or doubts about platform
- Prefers status quo; hesitant to change
- May be motivated by competitor pressure

**25 – Passive**
- Neutral; participating because asked
- No enthusiasm; waiting to see results
- Minimal proactive engagement

**50 – Neutral**
- Sees value but not urgent
- Will participate but not an advocate
- Standard adoption timeline expected

**75 – Interested**
- Expressed interest in benefits
- Willing to learn new system
- May promote internally once comfortable

**100 – Enthusiastic Advocate**
- Excited about platform benefits
- Proactively promoted to peers
- First-mover mentality; early adopter profile

### Readiness Tier Assignment

- **Tier 1 (80–100):** ✅ Ready for minimal onboarding
- **Tier 2 (60–79):** ✅ Ready with moderate support
- **Tier 3 (40–59):** ✅ Ready with high touch support
- **Tier 4 (<40):** ⚠️ Escalate to leadership (consider exclusion)

**Batch C (Learner):** Low-frequency + Tier 3 + edge-case testers (4–6 retailers) → Week 3–4 launch

## DIVERSITY VALIDATION CHECKLIST

Before finalizing batches, validate:

- [ ] Each batch contains ≥2 different frequency segments (avoid clustering)
- [ ] Geographic distribution balanced (e.g., <50% from single region per batch)
- [ ] Support readiness distributed (avoid concentrating all Tier 3 in one batch)
- [ ] Edge-case scenarios distributed (return logistics, multi-location, remote, etc.)
- [ ] Total pilot size: 25–40 retailers (typically 10–16 + 11–18 + 4–6)
- [ ] Fast-Track batch is 40–50% of total (confidence builder)
- [ ] Tier 4 retailers escalated to leadership (not forced into pilot)
