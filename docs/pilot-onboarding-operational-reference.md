# Pilot Onboarding Operational Reference

## Quick Reference: Batch Go-Live Schedule

### Timeline Overview (4 weeks)

```
WEEK -1 (Pre-Pilot)
├─ Mon: Pre-onboarding calls with all retailers (confirm readiness)
├─ Wed: Tech pre-flight tests (connectivity, credentials)
└─ Fri: Final readiness review & go/no-go decision

WEEK 0 (Kick-off Week)
├─ Batch A kick-offs: Mon–Tue (Fast-Track cohort, 10–16 retailers)
├─ Daily standup: 9am PT (all support staff)
└─ Issues triage: 4pm PT (daily)

WEEK 1 (Stabilization—Batch A)
├─ Mon: WBR #1 with Batch A (45 min × 3–4 calls)
├─ Wed: Batch B kicks off (Core cohort launches)
├─ Daily standups continue (Batch A + Batch B)
└─ Issues monitoring: Trending analysis

WEEK 2 (Stabilization—Batch A & B)
├─ Mon: WBR #2 with Batch A + B (consolidated view)
├─ Tue: Usage trend analysis (adoption curve check)
├─ Wed: Batch C cohort begins (Learner, staggered: 2–3 retailers/day)
├─ Daily standups continue (all batches)
└─ Training reinforcement calls (ad-hoc as needed)

WEEK 3 (Ramp-up—All Batches)
├─ Mon: WBR #3 all batches
├─ Tue: Performance review (system uptime, response times)
├─ Wed: Advanced feature training sessions (if applicable)
├─ Transition to on-demand support (move off 24/7 hotline)
└─ Begin "Business as Usual" support SLA

WEEK 4 (Transition & Learnings)
├─ Mon: Final WBR all batches + feedback synthesis
├─ Tue–Thu: 1:1 closing calls (thank you, feedback summary)
├─ Fri: Pilot retrospective with internal team
└─ Begin go/no-go assessment for full launch

POST-PILOT (Week 5+)
├─ Monthly business reviews (scaled)
├─ On-demand support (standard SLA)
└─ Learnings applied to Phase 2 launch (50–100 retailers)
```

## Daily Standup Agenda Template

**Standup:** Monday–Friday, 9:00–9:30 am PT (30 min)  
**Attendees:** PM, Product Lead, Support Manager, Tech Lead, Onboarding Owner

| Section | Time | Owner | Content |
|---------|------|-------|---------|
| **Adoption Metrics** | 5 min | Analytics | Logins, orders placed, conversion rate, volume |
| **Blockers** | 10 min | Support | Critical issues, support backlog, escalations |
| **Wins** | 3 min | Support | Success stories, quick resolutions, positive feedback |
| **Action Items** | 7 min | PM | Open tasks, dependencies, deadlines |
| **Next 24h Plan** | 5 min | PM | Scheduled calls, launches, go-lives |

**Example Script:**

---

**PM:** Good morning, team. Let's start with adoption metrics, [Analytics].

**Analytics:** "Batch A is tracking well—13 of 16 retailers logged in on Day 1 (81% adoption). Orders created: 11 of 16 (69%). Average time-to-first-order: 24 hours. No system errors; uptime 99.9%."

**PM:** Great start. [Support], any blockers?

**Support:** "Three support tickets open. Two are user-education issues (password reset, catalog navigation)—resolved via email or phone. One is a browser compatibility issue with Firefox—escalating to tech team now. Support queue is clear; no backlog."

**Product:** "We're monitoring performance closely. No degradation. Seeing expected traffic patterns."

**PM:** Excellent. Any quick wins?

**Support:** "Retailer #003 called this morning—very happy with the platform. They're already planning to use it for their second location order."

**PM:** That's fantastic—let's capture that as a testimonial later. [Tech], any infrastructure concerns?

**Tech:** "Clean. Database response times optimal. No alerts."

**PM:** Okay, actions for today: (1) [Support] close out Firefox compatibility ticket by EOD. (2) [Product] prepare Batch B kick-off decks. (3) [Onboarding] confirm Batch B retailer readiness calls for tomorrow. See everyone same time tomorrow."

## Support Issue Escalation Template

**Use this for tracking and categorizing issues during pilot**

### Issue Triage Log

| Issue ID | Retailer | Category | Severity | Description | Status | Assigned To | Created | Resolved | Resolution |
|----------|----------|----------|----------|-------------|--------|-------------|---------|----------|-----------|
| P-001 | RTL-002 | UX | Medium | Unable to locate "All Products" tab on catalog screen | Resolved | [Support Agent] | May 12 | May 12 | User trained on navigation; added quick-start video link |
| P-002 | RTL-005 | Bug | High | Orders submitted but not appearing in dashboard | Open | [Tech] | May 13 | TBD | Investigating data sync issue |
| P-003 | RTL-008 | Data | Critical | Order quantities showing incorrectly (doubled) | Escalated | [Product] | May 13 | TBD | Engineering investigating database sync |
| P-004 | RTL-010 | UX | Low | Font size too small; user requested accessibility option | Open | [Product] | May 13 | TBD | Added to roadmap for v1.1 |
| P-005 | RTL-003 | Integration | Medium | User wants to sync orders to their QuickBooks | Open | [Tech] | May 14 | TBD | Evaluating 3rd-party connector options |

### Issue Categories

- **Bug:** System malfunction; code issue
- **UX:** User experience friction; workflow confusion
- **Data:** Data integrity, sync, or consistency issue
- **Integration:** Third-party system connectivity
- **Infrastructure:** Performance, downtime, connectivity
- **Policy:** Feature request; current system doesn't support

### Severity Levels

- **Critical:** System down; data loss; unable to complete core workflow (SLA: 2h response, 4h resolution attempt)
- **High:** Major functionality broken; workaround exists (SLA: 4h response, 1 day resolution)
- **Medium:** Partial functionality; user can proceed with friction (SLA: 12h response, 3 days resolution)
- **Low:** Non-blocking; nice-to-have; cosmetic (SLA: 2 days response, end-of-sprint resolution)

## Weekly Business Review (WBR) Agenda

**Schedule:** Monday, 10:00–11:00 am PT (60 min per batch)  
**Attendees:** PM, Retailer Contact, Assigned Support Agent, Onboarding Owner

### Pre-WBR Preparation (by Friday prior)

- [ ] Pull adoption metrics for retailer (logins, orders, conversion rate)
- [ ] Summarize support tickets (open, closed, trends)
- [ ] Prepare performance baseline (system uptime, load times)
- [ ] Catalog feedback themes (from support calls, surveys)
- [ ] Identify "asks" for product/ops team

### WBR Agenda (60 min)

| Section | Time | Owner | Content |
|---------|------|-------|---------|
| **Opening** | 5 min | PM | Opening remarks; thank you for participation |
| **Data Review** | 15 min | PM | Adoption metrics, KPI progress, trend analysis |
| **Feedback Synthesis** | 15 min | Support | Support tickets summary, most common questions, pain points |
| **Wins & Learnings** | 10 min | Retailer | What's working well; suggested improvements |
| **Action Items** | 10 min | PM/Retailer | Commitments, next steps, support escalations |
| **Closing** | 5 min | PM | Next WBR date, thank you |

### WBR Outputs

After each WBR, PM publishes:
1. **Meeting notes** (shared with retailer for transparency)
2. **Action items tracker** (who owns what, deadline)
3. **Metrics snapshot** (one-pager with KPI status)

**Example WBR Output Email:**

---

**Subject:** Pilot WBR Recap – Retailer #002 (Batch A), Week 1

### Go/No-Go Voting

**Sign-off roles:**
- [ ] PM: Adoption & retailer health  
- [ ] Product: Feature readiness & UX  
- [ ] Tech: Infrastructure & performance  
- [ ] Support: Team capacity & satisfaction  
- [ ] Exec Sponsor: Business alignment  

**Decision:**
- ✅ **GO:** ≥4/5 votes + all success metrics green
- ⚠️ **GO WITH CONDITIONS:** 3/5 votes + some cautions; specific mitigations in place
- ❌ **NO-GO:** <3/5 votes or critical metrics red; requires root-cause analysis + re-assessment date
